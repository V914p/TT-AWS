> Here, you can freely share your personal opinions about your experience participating in the First Cloud Journey program. This will help the FCJ team improve any shortcomings based on the following aspects:

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**
The working environment at First Cloud Journey is friendly, open, and suitable for learning. During the program, I felt comfortable asking questions and discussing problems with other members. The community atmosphere helped me become more active in learning AWS, completing labs, and improving my project. I also appreciated that the environment encouraged self-learning instead of depending completely on instructions. In the future, I think it would be even better if there were more small group discussions or sharing sessions so interns could connect and exchange experiences more often.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**
The mentor and admin team provided helpful support throughout the internship. When I had difficulties with AWS labs, Unity online features, or project documentation, I was guided in a clear and practical way. The mentor did not only give direct answers but also encouraged me to think, test, and find the cause of problems by myself. This helped me improve my problem-solving ability. The admin team also supported the program schedule, event information, and required documents, making the internship process easier to follow.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**
The tasks during the internship were highly related to my major in Information Technology and Software Engineering. I had the opportunity to apply programming, system design, networking, cloud computing, and documentation skills into real practice. Besides AWS labs, I also connected the knowledge to my RoughLife Unity game project, especially in the areas of online multiplayer, dedicated server architecture, and cloud deployment planning. This made the internship more practical and meaningful for my study direction.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**
This internship gave me many opportunities to improve both technical and soft skills. On the technical side, I learned more about AWS services such as IAM, EC2, VPC, CloudWatch, Lambda, DynamoDB, S3, CloudFront, WAF, Cognito, and GameLift-related concepts. I also improved my Unity development skills, especially online gameplay, boss mechanics, weapon systems, and server deployment preparation. On the soft-skill side, I practiced writing reports, presenting project ideas, organizing worklogs, and communicating more clearly with team members.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**
The team culture is positive and supportive. Members are willing to share their knowledge, give feedback, and help each other when someone faces technical issues. I liked the fact that the program combined learning, events, workshops, and project development instead of focusing only on theory. This made me feel more motivated and gave me a better view of how a learning community can support students and young developers. Even though I was still learning, I felt encouraged to contribute and improve step by step.

**6. Internship Policies / Benefits**
The internship program provided a clear learning path, useful reference materials, and many hands-on activities. The workshops and events were valuable because they helped me understand cloud computing, AI, DevOps, teamwork, and career orientation from different perspectives. The flexibility of the program also allowed me to balance learning AWS, developing my Unity project, and preparing documentation. I think this is a good model for interns who want to gain both technical knowledge and real project experience.

### Additional Questions

* What did you find **most satisfying** during your internship?
  The most satisfying part of my internship was being able to connect the knowledge I learned from AWS with my own game project. I did not only study AWS services theoretically, but also planned how to use EC2, Lambda Function URL, DynamoDB, S3, CloudFront, WAF, Cognito, and other services for the RoughLife online multiplayer architecture. In addition, I was able to improve many Unity gameplay features such as online room flow, boss rooms, weapon systems, hitbox debugging, and dedicated server preparation.

* What do you think the company **should improve** for future interns?
  I think the program could improve by providing more sample project structures or reference templates for interns who are building their own cloud-based projects. Some AWS services can be difficult for beginners, so having more step-by-step project examples would help interns understand how to apply the labs to real systems. It would also be helpful to have more review sessions where interns can present their progress and receive feedback from mentors or other members.

* If recommending to a friend, would you **suggest they intern here**? Why or why not?
  Yes, I would recommend this internship program to my friends, especially those who want to learn AWS, cloud architecture, DevOps mindset, or cloud-based project deployment. The program provides a good balance between self-learning, hands-on practice, events, and community support. It is suitable for students who are willing to explore new technologies and improve their practical skills.

---

### Suggestions & Expectations

* Do you have any suggestions to improve the internship experience?
  I suggest adding more project-based guidance sessions where interns can learn how to design a complete architecture from the beginning, estimate costs, choose AWS services, and prepare evidence for reports. It would also be useful to have more checkpoints for reviewing project diagrams, deployment plans, and final documentation.

* Would you like to continue this program in the future?
  Yes, I would like to continue participating in similar programs in the future. Through this internship, I gained more confidence in learning AWS, building cloud architecture, and connecting cloud services with my Unity project. I believe continuing this type of program would help me improve further in cloud computing, backend systems, and online game deployment.

* Any other comments (free sharing):
  Overall, this internship was a meaningful experience for me. It helped me improve my technical skills, understand AWS services more clearly, and apply cloud knowledge to my RoughLife game project. I also learned the importance of documentation, teamwork, self-learning, and asking questions when facing problems. These experiences will be useful for my future career as a software developer.
